About

I’m Vincent Castigliola and my mission in life is to follow a very similar philosophy to that of the ancient stoics. If I can live life virtuously by learning new things, being courageous, treating people with kindness, and abstaining from instant gratifications, I will consider my life a success. I can think of no better way to sharing who I am than to explain exactly how I tackle each one of these virtues in my everyday life.

One of my favorite ways of acquiring wisdom is by reading novels. In a world where we are glued to our cell phones and computers half of the day, I welcome the moments I have where I can unwind on the couch away from technology and read a physical book. I'm also a sucker for podcasts. My favorite one is the Huberman Lab Podcast where host Andrew Huberman talks about science and science-based tools for optimal living. Another method I use to learn new things is conversing with as many people as possible on a given day. During most conversations, I tend to listen more than I would speak. The main reason for this being if I'm speaking than I'm not listening and if I'm not listening, then I'm not learning.

Courage is something I used to struggle with when it involved meeting new people. I was very much an introvert in my youth, but I learned how to overcome my reticent nature in my twenties while I was backpacking around Australia in 2013. When you're an introvert living the hostel life in a foreign country by yourself, you really have no other choice but to find the courage to form relationships with people. I found the fear of being alone and isolated to be an incredibly effective motivator. I made many friends during the experience. Some of which I still connect with to this day. I should also include preparing for adversity as another tool I use to develop courage. Whenever I first started going to Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu classes, there were always some fears that came to mind that made me want to stay home but all I had to do to overcome the fear was to identify the worst-case scenarios (getting injured, looking like an idiot) and prepare for them in advance (learn to pace myself, realize everyone has to start somewhere). It took me a long time before I really appreciated the value of having immense courage. Without it, you cannot grow, you cannot take risks, and you cannot really appreciate what your mind is capable of.

By nature (or perhaps Catholic upbringing), I always tended to err on the side of kindness. However, it took me a long time before I realized how important the quality of kindness really is on your emotional health and your community. Whenever I perform a sincere act of kindness such as expressing words of encouragement to a friend or giving a compliment to a stranger, it makes me feel good knowing I'm spreading positivity to others around me.

Eliminating or limiting instant gratifications has been a quality that I have enjoyed working on to a substantial degree. One reason I enjoy abstaining from pleasure seeking activities so much is the encouraging effect I feel from knowing I have the discipline to avoid such desires, especially the ones that I used to be addicted to. Some of these desires included video games, television, energy drinks, fast food, nicotine, and alcohol. I will partake in streaming shows and the occasional drink every now and then but the rest I had to give up entirely. I find spending my time pursuing activities that are meaningful to be far more rewarding.

Many of my personal beliefs on living a good life were heavily influenced by books that I have read. Some of these include Meditations by Marcus Aurelius, Stoicism and The Art of Happiness by Donald Robertson, and 12 Rules for Life by Jordan Peterson.

As a side note, I would also like to add that I'm currently on a mission to break into medical device sales. If you are interested in networking, feel free to reach out. You can find my contact information below.
